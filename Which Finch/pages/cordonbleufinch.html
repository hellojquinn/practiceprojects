The Cordon Bleu Finch

A slim, long-tailed, and pretty waxbill with its upper parts light brown/grey and underparts and tail a brilliant blue. Males have a distinct magenta coloured cheek, whereas females have the light brown/grey wrap under their chin like a bonnet. There is also a close relative known as the Blue-Capped Cordon Bleu Finch.

Geography

Cordon Bleu Finches are found in many countries across central and eastern Africa and are not migratory. It has also been introduced to the Hawaiian Islands of Hawaii and Oahu. They inhabit a wide variety of habitat including dry thorn scrub, all types of savanna, villages and cultivated areas.

Habits & Lifestyle

Usually found in pairs or small flocks or charms, and always close to a water source. The males sing a rhythmic series of high pitched notes with the last note being longer and lower in pitch, and unlike most other passerine type birds, the females will also sing but their song is less complex.

Quick Facts

  • This finch is considered one of the most exotic finches in aviculture.
  • These finches are known to feed occasionally on beeswax in the wild.
  • The patch on their cheeks can rarely be found as orange or even yellow.
