The Goldfinch

Also known as the European Goldfinch, native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. Males and females look similar with a red face, black and white head, brown and white body, with black and yellow wings, ivory-coloured bill which is long and pointed and a forked tail. Males red mask is darker and longer.

Geography

Despite where they are natively from, Goldfinches have been introduced all over the world from Australia to North America, and can be found in open woodlands, forest edges, thickets, marshy areas, grasslands, orchards, gardens and parks, and will make local movements around to avoid bad weather.

Habits & Lifestyle

Goldfinches are social birds especially in the winter when they form groups of up to 40 and will cover great distances daily for food. They're seen searching plant to plant for seeds, buds, thistles and fruits and will roost in groups on branches or in bushes at night. Their calls and song are melodic sounds of trills and twitters.

Quick Facts

  • Only females build nests whereas males defend while singing.
  • They're the only birds with long pointed beaks to get seeds from teasels.
  • If kept with canaries, they will adapt their song.
